Finding a capable walking shoe for used to mean sacrificing cushioning or durability. That’s no longer the case. Brands like ASICS and New Balance have brought genuine walking-specific features, . gel inserts, stable soles, and breathable uppers, . to their entry-level lines. The trick is knowing which details matter most for your foot shape and walking style. When shopping in this price range, focus on three things: midsole cushioning (enough to absorb shock without feeling spongey), outsole grip (especially if you walk on wet pavement or trails), and upper construction (mesh that breathes but still holds your foot securely). Avoid shoes that skimp on arch support or use flimsy foam that packs out quickly. The two picks below consistently earn praise from budget-conscious walkers who cover serious miles.

Best for specific needs

Best for all-around daily walking

If you walk a few miles most days on sidewalks or paved paths and want a shoe that balances cushioning, support, and price, the ASICS Gel-Contend 8 is the clear winner. Its gel pocket in the heel softens impact without making the shoe feel unstable, and the mesh upper keeps feet cool. Owner feedback consistently highlights how well it performs right out of the box. Our pick: ASICS Gel-Contend 8 Walking Shoe.

Best for wide feet or bunion relief

The New Balance 577’s classic last provides noticeably more room in the toe area than most budget shoes. Walkers with bunions, hammertoes, or simply wide feet report that the 577 doesn’t pinch or rub. The firm midsole also suits those who prefer a less plush ride and more ground feel. It’s a reliable choice for all-day wear during errands or long park strolls. Our pick: New Balance 577 Walking Shoe.

Best for value in long-term durability

While the ASICS Gel-Contend 8 offers softer cushioning, the New Balance 577 tends to hold its structure longer before the midsole compresses. The denser EVA foam and tougher outsole compound mean the 577 can still feel supportive after hundreds of miles. For budget shoppers who intend to wear the same shoe daily for a year or more, the 577 offers the better long-term value. Our pick: New Balance 577 Walking Shoe.

Our verdict

Both the ASICS Gel-Contend 8 and New Balance 577 prove that you don’t need to spend more than to get a competent walking shoe. The ASICS wins on plush comfort and light weight, making it the top pick for most people. The New Balance is the smarter choice if your feet need extra width or you want a shoe that holds its shape longer. Try each on if you can; fit is everything in this price range.
